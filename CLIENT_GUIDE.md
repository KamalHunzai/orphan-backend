# Orphan Mentorship Platform — User Guide

**Website:** https://mentoring.hasene.org

---

## Before You Begin — How Everything Connects

Understanding the order below is important. Each step depends on the one before it.

```
STEP 1 → SuperAdmin creates their own account (done once)
STEP 2 → SuperAdmin logs in and registers Mentors
STEP 3 → Mentor logs in and registers Children
STEP 4 → Mentor manages children (tasks, reports, visits, activities, journals)
STEP 5 → SuperAdmin monitors everything across all countries
```

> **You cannot register a child without a Mentor. You cannot create tasks or reports without a child. Follow the order.**

---

## Part 1 — Getting Into the System

### 1.1 Opening the Platform

Go to **https://mentoring.hasene.org**

You will see a screen with two buttons:

- **Mentor Portal** — for Mentors (field workers)
- **Management Portal** — for the SuperAdmin (platform manager)

Click the one that matches your role.

---

### 1.2 Logging In (Both Portals)

Both portals have the same login screen.

1. Enter your **Email address**
2. Enter your **Password**
3. Click **Sign In**

> **Where does my password come from?**
> Passwords are automatically generated and sent to your email when your account is created. Check your inbox (and spam folder).

---

### 1.3 Forgot Your Password?

1. On the login screen, click **Forgot Password**
2. Enter your registered email address
3. Check your email for a 6-digit OTP code
4. Enter the OTP code on the next screen
5. Enter and confirm your new password
6. Click **Reset Password**
7. Log in with your new password

---

## Part 2 — Management Portal (SuperAdmin)

> **Who uses this:** The SuperAdmin — the person responsible for overseeing the entire platform, all countries, all mentors, and all children.

---

### 2.1 Creating Your SuperAdmin Account (First Time Only)

> This only needs to be done once. Once created, the system does not allow a second SuperAdmin account.

1. Go to **https://mentoring.hasene.org**
2. Click **Management Portal**
3. On the login page, click **Create Account** (or the sign up link at the bottom)
4. Fill in the form:

| Field | What to enter |
|---|---|
| Full Name | Your full name |
| Email | Your email address — your password will be sent here |
| Phone Number | Optional |
| Country | Select your country |

5. Click **Create Account**
6. Check your email inbox for your **temporary password** (also check spam)
7. Return to the login page and sign in with your email and temporary password
8. Go to **Settings** and change your password immediately after first login

> **Note:** Only one SuperAdmin account can exist in the system. If you see the message *"A superadmin already exists"*, your account has already been created — go to the login page instead.

---

### 2.2 Dashboard

After logging in you land on the **Dashboard**. This gives you an instant overview of the platform:

- **Total Orphans** — total number of registered children
- **Pending Reports** — reports not yet reviewed
- **Upcoming Schedule** — visits planned for the near future
- **Urgent Cases** — children flagged as urgent

Use the **country selector** (top right of the screen) to filter everything by country — Serbia, Skopje, or Sri Lanka.

> Changing the country updates all numbers, lists, and charts on every page.

---

### 2.3 Registering a Mentor

> **Dependency:** You must do this before any children can be registered. Mentors are the field workers who manage children day-to-day.

**Steps:**

1. Click **Mentors** in the left sidebar
2. Click the **Register New Mentor** button (top right)
3. Fill in the form:

**Personal Information**
| Field | What to enter |
|---|---|
| Full Name | Mentor's full legal name |
| Email | A valid email — login credentials will be sent here |
| Phone Number | 10–15 digit number, no spaces |
| Country | Serbia / Skopje / Sri Lanka |

**Professional Information**
| Field | What to enter |
|---|---|
| Employment Type | Paid or Volunteer |
| Years of Experience | Number of years in social/welfare work |
| Professional Background | Brief description of qualifications |

**Assignments**
| Field | What to enter |
|---|---|
| Maximum Case Load | How many children this mentor can handle |
| Preferred Age Group | 5–8 / 9–12 / 13–16 / All ages |

**Additional Information**
| Field | What to enter |
|---|---|
| Special Skills | Any certifications or relevant skills |

4. Click **Register Mentor**
5. The mentor will receive their login credentials by email automatically

> **After registration:** The mentor appears in your Mentors list. You can click their name to view their full profile and how many children are assigned to them.

---

### 2.4 Viewing Mentors

1. Click **Mentors** in the sidebar
2. The list shows all mentors for the selected country
3. Each row shows: Name, Email, Phone, Employment Type, Country, number of assigned children
4. Click any mentor to open their **full profile** — you can see their professional background, case load, and all children assigned to them

---

### 2.5 Viewing Children

> **Dependency:** Children are registered by Mentors, not by the SuperAdmin. They appear here once a Mentor has added them.

1. Click **Children** in the sidebar
2. The list shows all children for the selected country
3. Click any child to see their full profile including health, education, financial situation, and assigned mentor

---

### 2.6 Reports

> **Dependency:** Reports are submitted by Mentors. They appear here after a Mentor submits one.

1. Click **Reports** in the sidebar
2. You can see all reports across all mentors for the selected country
3. Reports are colour-coded by urgency:
   - **Normal** — routine check-in
   - **Important** — needs attention
   - **Urgent** — requires prompt action
   - **Emergency** — immediate response required
4. Click any report to read the full details

> As SuperAdmin you can also submit a report on behalf of a mentor by clicking **New Report** and selecting both a child and a mentor from the dropdowns.

---

### 2.7 Tasks

> **Dependency:** Tasks require a child and a mentor to exist first.

1. Click **Tasks** in the sidebar
2. View all tasks created for children across the platform
3. To create a task yourself, click **Create New Task**:
   - Select the **child**
   - Select the **mentor** to assign it to
   - Fill in title, description, due date, priority, and type
   - Click **Create Task**

---

### 2.8 Activities

> **Dependency:** Activities are linked to a child and a mentor. Both must exist.

1. Click **Activities** in the sidebar
2. View all recorded activities (visits, media uploads, reports, other)
3. To record a new activity, click **Record Activity**:
   - Select the **Activity Type** (Visit / Report / Media Upload / Other)
   - Select the **Child**
   - Select the **Mentor**
   - Enter title, description, date, time, status
   - Attach files if needed (images, PDFs)
   - Click **Record Activity**

---

### 2.9 Media Gallery

1. Click **Media** in the sidebar
2. View all uploaded documents, videos, images, and audio files
3. To upload new material, click **Upload Media**:
   - Drag and drop a file or click the upload zone to browse
   - Enter a **Title** (required)
   - Enter a **Description** (required, at least 10 characters)
   - Add a **Tag** (e.g. Health, Education) — optional
   - Set a **Due Date** (required)
   - Select **Priority** and **Type**
   - Select the **Mentor** to assign it to
   - Click **Upload**

---

### 2.10 Visit Schedule

1. Click **Schedule** in the sidebar
2. View all planned visits for the selected country
3. To plan a visit, click **Plan Visit**:
   - Select the **Child**
   - Select the **Mentor**
   - Set the **Visit Date** and **Time**
   - Select **Visit Type** (Home Visit, School, Medical, Community)
   - Add notes if needed
   - Click **Save**

---

### 2.11 Announcements

Announcements are broadcast messages sent to all mentors. When you post one, every mentor receives a notification automatically.

1. Click **Announcements** in the sidebar
2. Click **New Announcement**
3. Enter a **Title**
4. Write the **Content**
5. Set the **Priority** (Normal / Important / Urgent)
6. Click **Post Announcement**

All mentors will see it immediately in their Announcements section and receive a notification.

---

### 2.12 Statistics

1. Click **Statistics** in the sidebar
2. View charts and graphs:
   - Reports by country
   - Age group distribution of children
   - Monthly activity trends
   - Task type breakdown
3. Use the country selector to filter data

---

### 2.13 Management Users

This section lets you add supporting management staff.

1. Click **Management Users** in the sidebar
2. Click **Add User**
3. Fill in name, email, phone, country, and role
4. Click **Save**

---

### 2.14 Notifications

1. Click the **Notifications** icon in the sidebar
2. See all system notifications — new registrations, reports submitted, tasks created, announcements
3. Notifications are marked as read automatically when viewed

---

### 2.15 Settings (SuperAdmin Profile)

1. Click **Settings** in the sidebar
2. Update your **Full Name** or **Email**
3. To change your password:
   - Enter your **Current Password**
   - Enter your **New Password**
   - Click **Save Changes**

---

## Part 3 — Mentor Portal

> **Who uses this:** Mentors (field workers) assigned by the SuperAdmin. They manage children day-to-day and submit all records.

---

### 3.1 Dashboard

After logging in you see the Mentor Dashboard:

- **Active Children** — number of children assigned to you
- **Reports This Month** — how many reports you have submitted
- **Upcoming Visits** — scheduled visits coming up
- **Learning Materials** — media uploaded by you

---

### 3.2 Registering a Child

> **Dependency:** You must be logged in as a Mentor. The child will be automatically assigned to you.

1. Click **Children** in the sidebar
2. Click **Add New Child**
3. Fill in the form:

**Basic Information** (Required fields marked with \*)
| Field | What to enter |
|---|---|
| First Name \* | Child's first name |
| Last Name \* | Child's last name |
| Age \* | Child's age in years |
| Gender \* | Male / Female |
| Location \* | Country where the child is based |
| Language \* | Child's primary language |

**Guardian Information**
| Field | What to enter |
|---|---|
| Guardian Name \* | Name of the guardian or caregiver |
| Relationship \* | e.g. Uncle, Grandmother, Foster Parent |
| Contact Number \* | Guardian's phone number |
| Email | Child's email (used for their own login) |

**Health & Education**
| Field | What to enter |
|---|---|
| General Condition | Brief description of physical health |
| Education Level | Current grade or school level |
| School Performance | Academic standing |
| Psychological Support Needs | Any known mental health or support needs |

**Financial & Additional**
| Field | What to enter |
|---|---|
| Financial Situation | Brief description of family finances |
| Additional Notes | Anything else relevant |
| Profile Picture | Upload a photo of the child |
| Document | Upload any supporting document |

4. Click **Register Child**
5. The child's login credentials are sent to their email automatically

> After registering, the child appears in **My Children** list and you can click their name to view or edit their profile.

---

### 3.3 Child Profile

Click any child's name to open their profile. You will see tabs for:

- **Overview** — personal and health details
- **Tasks** — tasks assigned to this child
- **Reports** — welfare reports submitted for this child
- **Journals** — journal entries written about this child
- **Visit History** — past and upcoming visits
- **Activities** — all recorded activities

---

### 3.4 Creating a Task

> **Dependency:** A child must be registered first.

1. Click **Tasks** in the sidebar
2. Click **Create New Task**
3. Fill in:
   - **Task Title** — what needs to be done
   - **Description** — more details (optional)
   - **Related Child** — select from your assigned children
   - **Due Date** — when it must be completed
   - **Due Time** — optional time of day
   - **Priority** — Low / Medium / High
   - **Task Type** — Visit / Report / Document / Other
4. Click **Create Task**

---

### 3.5 Submitting a Report

> **Dependency:** A child must be registered. Reports are a key record of the child's welfare.

1. Click **Reports** in the sidebar
2. Click **New Report**
3. Select the **Child** from the dropdown
4. Fill in the sections:

**Basic**
- Report Type (Regular Check-in / Monthly Report / Emergency Report / Special Event)
- Urgency Level (Normal / Important / Urgent / Emergency)

**Health Status**
- General Condition, Recent Doctor Visits, Nutrition Status, Physical Activities

**Educational Progress**
- Academic Performance, Attendance & Participation

**Emotional Development**
- Mental State, Social Integration

**Financial Situation**
- Current Needs, Existing Support

**Special Events**
- Notable Events or Achievements

**Additional Notes**
- Any other observations

5. Click **Submit Report**

> A notification is automatically sent confirming the report was received.

---

### 3.6 Recording an Activity

> **Dependency:** A child must be registered.

1. Click **Activities** in the sidebar
2. Click **Record Activity**
3. Fill in:
   - **Activity Type** — Visit / Report / Media Upload / Other
   - **Related Child** — select the child
   - **Activity Title** — brief name for the activity
   - **Description** — what happened
   - **Date** and **Time**
   - **Status** — Completed / Pending / Cancelled
   - **Attachments** — upload photos or PDF files (optional)
4. Click **Record Activity**

---

### 3.7 Planning a Visit

1. Click **Planning** in the sidebar
2. Click **Plan Visit**
3. Fill in:
   - **Child** — select from your assigned children
   - **Visit Date** — when the visit will take place
   - **Visit Time** — time of day
   - **Visit Type** — Home Visit / School / Medical / Community
   - **Notes** — purpose of the visit or anything to prepare
4. Click **Save**

The visit appears in your schedule and in the SuperAdmin's schedule view.

---

### 3.8 Writing a Journal Entry

Journals are private observations and notes about a child's emotional and psychological state.

> **Dependency:** A child must be registered.

1. Click the child's name to open their **Profile**
2. Go to the **Journals** tab
3. Click **New Journal Entry**
4. Fill in:
   - **Title** — subject of the entry
   - **Journal Text** — your full observations and notes
   - **Mood Rating** — 1 (very low) to 10 (very high)
   - **Emotion Tags** — e.g. Happy, Anxious, Withdrawn
   - **Social Interaction** — how the child is interacting with others
   - **Assessment Type** — type of assessment done
   - **Activities** — activities the child participated in
   - **File Attachment** — attach any supporting document
5. Click **Save Entry**

---

### 3.9 Uploading Media

1. Click **Media** in the sidebar
2. Click **Upload Media**
3. Drag and drop your file or click to browse
4. Fill in the title, description, tag, due date, and type
5. Click **Upload**

Files are stored securely and accessible to the SuperAdmin.

---

### 3.10 Viewing Announcements

1. Click **Announcements** in the sidebar
2. Read any messages posted by the SuperAdmin
3. Announcements are colour-coded by priority

---

### 3.11 Notifications

1. Click **Notifications** in the sidebar
2. View all system alerts — task reminders, report confirmations, new announcements, child registrations

---

### 3.12 Edit Profile

1. Click **Edit Profile** or **Profile** in the sidebar
2. Update your name, phone, professional background, or profile photo
3. To change your password, enter your current password and new password in the Settings section
4. Click **Save**

## Part 5 — Common Questions

**Q: I did not receive my password email.**
Check your spam/junk folder. If it is not there, ask your SuperAdmin to re-register your account with the same email.

**Q: I registered a child but they do not appear in the list.**
Make sure you are looking at the correct country in the country selector (top right of the screen).

**Q: Can I edit a child's details after registering?**
Yes. Click the child's name, then click Edit on their profile page.

**Q: Can I delete a child or mentor?**
Records are never permanently deleted — they are archived. Archived records are hidden from lists but the data is preserved.

**Q: The report urgency is set to Emergency — what happens?**
The report is flagged and appears at the top of the SuperAdmin's urgent cases list. The SuperAdmin receives a notification immediately.

**Q: Can two mentors share the same child?**
No. Each child is assigned to one mentor at a time. To reassign, the SuperAdmin must update the child's profile.
